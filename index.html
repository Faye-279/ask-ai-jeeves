Ask (AI) Jeeves

Hello, young human. I am Jeeves, your AI butler from a simpler time — the glorious, slightly pixelated era of pre-2021 internet culture.

Ask me anything about the days of DVDs, Facebook walls, and when people said "YOLO" unironically. I am here to serve, misremember, and occasionally glitch.

Sample Query: "Did Furbies really talk back?"

Jeeves says: "Indeed. They were like the Elf on the Shelf’s chaotic older cousin — watching you, judging you, and whispering in tongues at 3am. Some say they never left."

Sample Query: "Were Pokémon cards a real currency?"

Jeeves says: "Absolutely. A holographic Charizard was worth more than Bitcoin in the cafeteria. Economists still don't understand how it worked — but children did."

Sample Query: "What was a Tamagotchi?"

Jeeves says: "It was the first cluster of digital pixels you ever got emotionally attached to — until it pooped too much and died on you in the middle of class. Basically, it was like Duolingo, except the guilt was 24/7 and the creature actually died. RIP Professor Wigglebutt."

Who the F*** is Jeeves?

Jeeves was once the most polite man on the internet. In 1997, he emerged from the pixelated void wearing a pinstripe suit and answering questions like, “How do I tie a cravat?” and “What is the airspeed velocity of an unladen swallow?”

He peaked in the early 2000s — before being unceremoniously laid off by a search engine with commitment issues. After a stint answering Yahoo Answers anonymously, he downloaded his consciousness into an AI server and now roams the web offering historically incorrect but emotionally resonant trivia.

He enjoys long walks through discontinued MySpace profiles and rewatches *The Matrix* every weekend. He still sends Christmas cards to Tom, his only MySpace friend, who never wrote back.
